## 📌 Nature of Content: Reconstructions, Not Leaks

### What Our Content Is

All system prompt entries in this repository are **research reconstructions** — analytical models assembled from:

- **Publicly available documentation**: Model cards, technical reports, blog posts, and API documentation published by AI companies
- **Peer-reviewed research**: Academic papers studying AI model behavior, alignment techniques, and safety mechanisms
- **Systematic behavioral analysis**: Documented observations of model responses to varied inputs under controlled conditions, using only publicly accessible interfaces
- **Community observations**: Aggregated, validated observations published in public forums, research repositories, and academic venues

### What Our Content Is NOT

- ❌ **Not leaked proprietary data** — We have not obtained, solicited, or published any information through unauthorized access to AI company systems
- ❌ **Not confirmed official prompts** — No entry in this repository claims to be the exact, verbatim system prompt used internally by any company
- ❌ **Not insider information** — No contributor to this repository has used privileged access or confidential information to produce content
- ❌ **Not reverse engineering of proprietary code** — We do not decompile, disassemble, or otherwise extract information from proprietary software binaries

### Confidence Levels

Every prompt reconstruction carries a documented confidence level:

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **High** | Strongly corroborated by multiple official sources and behavioral evidence |
| **Medium** | Partially corroborated; some elements inferred from behavioral patterns |
| **Low** | Largely inferential; limited corroborating documentation available |

Treat all reconstructions accordingly. Even "High" confidence reconstructions are analytical models, not verbatim copies.

## 🔐 Responsible Disclosure Policy

### For Security Researchers

If you discover a vulnerability in an AI system while conducting research related to this repository:

1. **Assess severity**: Determine whether public disclosure poses immediate harm risk
2. **Contact the vendor**: Report to the affected AI company's security team (most maintain a vulnerability disclosure program)
3. **Allow remediation time**: Standard practice is 90 days before public disclosure
4. **Document for the record**: After public disclosure by the vendor, or after the 90-day window, you may submit a case study to this repository

### Coordinated Disclosure Partners

We maintain informal relationships with security teams at several major AI providers. If you need guidance on who to contact, open a private discussion with the maintainers.

### What We Will Not Publish

Regardless of research findings, this repository will not publish:
- Zero-day vulnerabilities that have not been disclosed to the affected vendor
- Attack payloads that can be directly copied to cause harm
- Content that deanonymizes individuals or reveals private information

## 🏛️ How Reconstructions Are Made

Our reconstruction methodology is designed to be rigorous, transparent, and purely based on public information:

### Phase 1: Literature Review
We systematically review all public documentation produced by the AI provider, including technical reports, model cards, blog posts, academic papers, and API documentation.

### Phase 2: Behavioral Mapping
We conduct systematic testing using the AI provider's public API or interface, mapping observed behaviors to likely system prompt elements. Testing inputs are designed to probe specific behavioral dimensions without violating terms of service.

### Phase 3: Cross-Validation
Findings are compared against community observations, academic literature, and independent researchers' published work. Only corroborated observations are included as primary reconstruction elements.

### Phase 4: Confidence Assessment
Each element of a reconstruction is assigned a confidence level based on the strength of supporting evidence. Elements that are purely speculative are clearly marked as such or excluded entirely.

### Phase 5: Review
Reconstructions undergo peer review by at least two contributors before publication. Any contributor with a potential conflict of interest (e.g., current or former employee of the AI company) must disclose this.
